In sound transit ridership data (Puget Sound Consolidated)  there is: 
  Small files called agency, areas, fare attributes, feed info, modifications, transfers, translations 
    Areas, feed info, modifications, transfers, and possibly fare attributes seem the most useful small files. 
  calendar_dates, calendar, fare rules, routes, are more medium sized (100s of lines) and seem useful. 
  Shapes, stop_times, stops, trips, trips, seem very useful and are the largest (>10,000 lines). 
In the Sound Transit GTFS schedule data there is: 
  Most files are in "small" (<100 lines), most useful seem to be: 
    areas, calendar_dates, calendar, fare_attributes, fare_transfer_rules, modifications, networks, rider_categories, route_networks, routes, stop_areas, stop_times, stops, shapes 
  Many more files present, smaller but attribution might be more granular. Might need different setups for each source.
